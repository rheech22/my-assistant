# House of Cards TV Show Overview

## General Information
House of Cards is an American political thriller television series created by Beau Willimon. It is based on the 1989 novel of the same title by Michael Dobbs and is an adaptation of the 1990 British series of the same name by Andrew Davies. The first season, consisting of 13 episodes, was released on February 1, 2013, on Netflix, marking it as the first TV series produced by a studio for the streaming service.

## Plot Summary
Set in Washington, D.C., the series follows Frank Underwood (played by Kevin Spacey), an amoral politician and Democrat from South Carolina's 5th congressional district. After being passed over for the appointment as Secretary of State, Frank, who remains House Majority Whip, initiates an elaborate plan to attain power, aided by his equally ambitious wife, Claire Underwood (played by Robin Wright). The series explores themes of ruthless pragmatism, manipulation, betrayal, and the quest for power.

## Reception and Awards
House of Cards received critical acclaim and numerous award nominations, including 33 Primetime Emmy Award nominations, with notable nominations for Outstanding Drama Series, Outstanding Lead Actor for Spacey, and Outstanding Lead Actress for Wright. It was the first original online-only streaming television series to receive major Emmy nominations. The show also garnered eight Golden Globe Award nominations, with Wright winning for Best Actress – Television Series Drama in 2014 and Spacey winning for Best Actor – Television Series Drama in 2015.

## Controversies
In 2017, following allegations of sexual misconduct against Kevin Spacey, Netflix terminated their relationship with him. The sixth and final season was produced and released in 2018 without his involvement.

## Adaptation Background
The American version of House of Cards is rooted in Michael Dobbs' 1989 novel, which provided the initial framework for the narrative. The British adaptation aired in 1990 and served as a stepping stone for the American version. The American series retains the core themes of political ambition and intrigue while introducing new characters and a complex narrative set in Washington, D.C.

## Conclusion
House of Cards stands out as a significant work in the realm of political dramas, showcasing the dark side of political maneuvering and the lengths individuals will go to achieve power.
